<p><b>Michael Lee Capel</b> (born October 13, 1961) is a former <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a> (MLB) right-handed <a href="/wiki/Pitcher" title="Pitcher">pitcher</a> who played for the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Cubs" title="Chicago Cubs">Chicago Cubs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee_Brewers" title="Milwaukee Brewers">Milwaukee Brewers</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Houston_Astros" title="Houston Astros">Houston Astros</a>. In 49 career games, Capel pitched 62.1 <a href="/wiki/Innings_pitched" title="Innings pitched">innings</a>, <a href="/wiki/Strikeout" title="Strikeout">struck out</a> 43 <a href="/wiki/Batting_(baseball)" title="Batting (baseball)">batters</a>, and had a career <a href="/wiki/Win%E2%80%93loss_record_(pitching)" title="Win–loss record (pitching)">win–loss record</a> of 3–4 with a 4.62 <a href="/wiki/Earned_run_average" title="Earned run average">earned run average</a> (ERA). While he played in MLB, Capel stood at 6&#160;feet 1&#160;inch (185&#160;cm) and weighed 175 pounds (79&#160;kg). A <a href="/wiki/Starting_pitcher" title="Starting pitcher">starting pitcher</a> in college and parts of his <a href="/wiki/Minor_league_baseball" class="mw-redirect" title="Minor league baseball">minor league baseball</a> career, he converted to <a href="/wiki/Relief_pitcher" title="Relief pitcher">relief pitching</a> while in Chicago's minor league system.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Phillies" title="Philadelphia Phillies">Philadelphia Phillies</a> chose Capel in the 24th round of the <a href="/wiki/1980_Major_League_Baseball_draft" title="1980 Major League Baseball draft">1980 MLB draft</a>, but Capel did not sign with the team: instead, he opted to attend the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Texas">University of Texas</a>. Capel played on the 1982 USA College All-Star Team, which competed in the <a href="/wiki/1982_Amateur_World_Series" title="1982 Amateur World Series">Amateur World Series</a> in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a> and placed third. The next year, Capel and the <a href="/wiki/Texas_Longhorns_baseball" title="Texas Longhorns baseball">Texas Longhorns</a> won the <a href="/wiki/College_World_Series" title="College World Series">College World Series</a>. After he was drafted by the Cubs, Capel left Texas and signed to play professional baseball; he played in six seasons of <a href="/wiki/Minor_league_baseball" class="mw-redirect" title="Minor league baseball">minor league baseball</a> before he made his MLB debut in 1988. Capel spent the entire 1989 season in <a href="/wiki/Triple-A_(baseball)" title="Triple-A (baseball)">Triple-A</a>, one level below the majors, but the Cubs released him at the end of the year. He agreed to terms with the Brewers and played in MLB after an injury opened a spot on Milwaukee's roster, but was again released at the end of the season. A free agent, the Astros signed Capel, and over the course of the season he pitched in 25 games for the team. He spent the final part of his career in the Astros <a href="/wiki/Farm_team" title="Farm team">farm system</a>, and after he made the 1992 Triple-A <a href="/wiki/All-star_game" title="All-star game">All-Star team</a>, Capel played his last season in 1993. After retirement, Capel worked as the general manager of a car dealership in <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston, Texas</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span></h2>
<p>Capel was born on October 13, 1961, in <a href="/wiki/Marshall,_Texas" title="Marshall, Texas">Marshall, Texas</a>, and attended <a href="/wiki/Spring_High_School" title="Spring High School">Spring High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Harris_County,_Texas" title="Harris County, Texas">Harris County</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Bref_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bref-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> As a child, he enjoyed watching the Astros play on weekends.<sup id="cite_ref-All_Star_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-All_Star-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> During his senior year in 1979, the Spring Lions won the AAAA <a href="/wiki/Athletic_conference" title="Athletic conference">conference</a> state championship, and Capel was named to the All-State team, composed of the best high school players in the state.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He set several pitching records at Spring High School.<sup id="cite_ref-USA_Today_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USA_Today-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Future teammate <a href="/wiki/Calvin_Schiraldi" title="Calvin Schiraldi">Calvin Schiraldi</a> called Capel a "hard thrower when he came out of high school" and "the top guy out of the state in 1980";<sup id="cite_ref-High_School_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-High_School-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Roger_Clemens" title="Roger Clemens">Roger Clemens</a> said he was "probably the best pitcher in the state at that time".<sup id="cite_ref-USA_Today_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USA_Today-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The Philadelphia Phillies <a href="/wiki/Draft_(sports)" title="Draft (sports)">drafted</a> Capel with the 605th overall pick in the 1980 MLB Draft, but instead of signing with the Phillies, he chose to attend the University of Texas.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Collegiate_career">Collegiate career</span></h2>
<p>Capel enrolled at the University of Texas in 1981, but did not play baseball, as stretched ligaments in his elbow forced him to miss his freshman season. Capel pitched <a href="/wiki/Sidearm_(baseball)" title="Sidearm (baseball)">sidearm</a> for the remainder of his collegiate career to compensate for a loss in the velocity of his <a href="/wiki/Fastball" title="Fastball">fastball</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-High_School_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-High_School-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 1982, he was named to the All-<a href="/wiki/Southwest_Conference" title="Southwest Conference">Southwest Conference</a> team and pitched to a 9–0 <a href="/wiki/Win%E2%80%93loss_record_(pitching)" title="Win–loss record (pitching)">win–loss record</a> with a 3.68 ERA, as the Longhorns finished 59–6.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Hist01_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hist01-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> His 1982 winning percentage of 1.000 ties him with 10 others for the best single-season winning percentage in Longhorns history (with a minimum of nine <a href="/wiki/Decision_(baseball)" class="mw-redirect" title="Decision (baseball)">decisions</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-Tex_Rec_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tex_Rec-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/1982_College_World_Series" class="mw-redirect" title="1982 College World Series">1982 College World Series</a>, Texas defeated the <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_State_Cowboys_baseball" title="Oklahoma State Cowboys baseball">Oklahoma State Cowboys</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Stanford_Cardinal_baseball" title="Stanford Cardinal baseball">Stanford Cardinal</a>, but losses to the <a href="/wiki/Miami_Hurricanes_baseball" title="Miami Hurricanes baseball">Miami Hurricanes</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Wichita_State_Shockers_baseball" title="Wichita State Shockers baseball">Wichita State Shockers</a> eliminated them from play per the tournament's <a href="/wiki/Double-elimination_tournament" title="Double-elimination tournament">double-elimination</a> format.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Capel played on the United States team in the <a href="/wiki/1982_Amateur_World_Series" title="1982 Amateur World Series">1982 Amateur World Series</a> overseen by the <a href="/wiki/International_Baseball_Federation" title="International Baseball Federation">International Baseball Federation</a>. Starting against Australia, he pitched a 14–4 <a href="/wiki/Complete_game" title="Complete game">complete game</a> victory, as the match ended after seven innings per the championship's <a href="/wiki/Ten-run_rule" class="mw-redirect" title="Ten-run rule">ten-run rule</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The United States eventually placed third in the competition.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The 1983 Longhorns finished with a 61–14 record and were ranked as the number one team in the nation by <i><a href="/wiki/Collegiate_Baseball_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="Collegiate Baseball Magazine">Collegiate Baseball Magazine</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> That season, the Texas team featured four future MLB pitchers: Capel, Clemens, <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Ruffin_(baseball)" title="Bruce Ruffin (baseball)">Bruce Ruffin</a>, and Schiraldi.<sup id="cite_ref-Stewart_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stewart-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/1983_College_World_Series" class="mw-redirect" title="1983 College World Series">1983 College World Series</a>, Capel allowed four <a href="/wiki/Hit_(baseball)" title="Hit (baseball)">hits</a> in a complete game against the <a href="/wiki/Michigan_Wolverines_baseball" title="Michigan Wolverines baseball">Michigan Wolverines</a> as the Longhorns advanced to the World Series finals.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Facing the <a href="/wiki/Alabama_Crimson_Tide_baseball" title="Alabama Crimson Tide baseball">Alabama Crimson Tide</a>, the Longhorns made Clemens their <a href="/wiki/Starting_pitcher" title="Starting pitcher">starting pitcher</a> and won 4–3.<sup id="cite_ref-Stewart_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stewart-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The championship was the fourth World Series title in University of Texas history, and their first since 1975.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Capel finished the season with a 13–1 record and a 2.98 ERA.<sup id="cite_ref-Hist01_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hist01-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> His career winning percentage (.957) ties him with Rick Burley for the fifth-best in Texas Longhorns history.<sup id="cite_ref-Tex_Rec_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tex_Rec-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In June, before the College World Series, he was drafted by the Cubs with the 320th overall pick in the <a href="/wiki/1983_Major_League_Baseball_draft" title="1983 Major League Baseball draft">1983 MLB draft</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional_career">Professional career</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chicago_Cubs">Chicago Cubs</span></h3>
<p>After he signed a professional contract with the Cubs, Capel no longer threw sidearmed.<sup id="cite_ref-High_School_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-High_School-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Capel began professional baseball with the <a href="/wiki/Class_A_(baseball)" class="mw-redirect" title="Class A (baseball)">Class A</a> <a href="/wiki/Quad_Cities_River_Bandits" title="Quad Cities River Bandits">Quad Cities Cubs</a>, but was promoted to the <a href="/wiki/Double-A_(baseball)" title="Double-A (baseball)">Double-A</a> <a href="/wiki/Midland_RockHounds" title="Midland RockHounds">Midland Cubs</a> shortly into the season. Over 11 games, he finished with a 4–3 record and a 3.51 ERA.<sup id="cite_ref-Minors_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Minors-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In 1984, Capel split playing time between the <a href="/wiki/Class_A-Advanced" class="mw-redirect" title="Class A-Advanced">Class A-Advanced</a> <a href="/wiki/Lodi_Crushers" title="Lodi Crushers">Lodi Crushers</a> and Midland; he led Midland in losses (10) and <a href="/wiki/Wild_pitch" title="Wild pitch">wild pitches</a> (11) and had the second-worst ERA (6.31) on the team.<sup id="cite_ref-Midland1984_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Midland1984-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> With Midland, Capel started 11 games out of the 16 in which he appeared.<sup id="cite_ref-Minors_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Minors-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> On his 0–7 record for the Crushers, Tom Alexander of the <i><a href="/wiki/Lodi_News-Sentinel" title="Lodi News-Sentinel">Lodi News-Sentinel</a></i> wrote that Capel's luck "has been all bad when it comes to wins".<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Both Lodi and Midland finished their seasons with losing records.<sup id="cite_ref-Midland1984_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Midland1984-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From 1985 to 1986, he played for the Double-A <a href="/wiki/Pittsfield_Cubs" title="Pittsfield Cubs">Pittsfield Cubs</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_League_(baseball)" title="Eastern League (baseball)">Eastern League</a>. In 1986, Capel only <a href="/wiki/Relief_pitcher" title="Relief pitcher">pitched in relief</a>, had a career-best 1.87 ERA over 62.2 <a href="/wiki/Innings_pitched" title="Innings pitched">innings pitched</a>, and led his team with 13 <a href="/wiki/Save_(baseball)" title="Save (baseball)">saves</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> After he was promoted to the <a href="/wiki/Triple-A_(baseball)" title="Triple-A (baseball)">Triple-A</a> <a href="/wiki/Iowa_Cubs" title="Iowa Cubs">Iowa Cubs</a>, Capel split time between <a href="/wiki/Starting_pitcher" title="Starting pitcher">starting</a> and relieving roles in 1987.<sup id="cite_ref-High_School_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-High_School-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> At 7–10, Capel tied for Iowa's lead in wins and losses, and led the team in <a href="/wiki/Strikeout" title="Strikeout">strikeouts</a>, with 75; his seven wins became a career high, and his loss total matched a career high set in Midland three years earlier.<sup id="cite_ref-Minors_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Minors-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> After the season, Ray Sons of the <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Sun-Times" title="Chicago Sun-Times">Chicago Sun-Times</a></i> said Capel "may be ready for middle relief in Chicago", and Dave van Dyck listed him as a <a href="/wiki/Prospect_(sports)" title="Prospect (sports)">prospect</a> for the 1988 season.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> During winter, Capel, <a href="/wiki/Greg_Maddux" title="Greg Maddux">Greg Maddux</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manny_Trillo" title="Manny Trillo">Manny Trillo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Damon_Berryhill" title="Damon Berryhill">Damon Berryhill</a>, and several other Cubs played for teams in the Caribbean. Capel played for the <a href="/wiki/%C3%81guilas_del_Zulia" title="Águilas del Zulia">Águilas del Zulia</a> of Venezuela and led the team with five saves.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:25%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"Honestly, it doesn't feel that good. I didn't deserve the win. If I would have done my job, it would have been Al [Nipper]'s win. I never dreamed my first win would be like that."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">— Mike Capel on his first career MLB win<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Capel played in <a href="/wiki/Spring_training" title="Spring training">spring training</a> ball as he tried to make Chicago's opening day roster.<sup id="cite_ref-High_School_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-High_School-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> After the Cubs acquired <a href="/wiki/Mike_Bielecki" title="Mike Bielecki">Mike Bielecki</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates" title="Pittsburgh Pirates">Pittsburgh Pirates</a> on March 31, 1988, they assigned Capel to Triple-A despite a <i>Sun-Times</i> prediction that he would be on the MLB roster.<sup id="cite_ref-April_1_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-April_1-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Prior to the acquisition, when <a href="/wiki/Al_Nipper" title="Al Nipper">Al Nipper</a> was supposed to become the Cubs' fifth starter, Capel was to take his spot in the <a href="/wiki/Bullpen" title="Bullpen">bullpen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-April_1_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-April_1-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The Cubs recalled Capel and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Grace" title="Mark Grace">Mark Grace</a> to the majors on May 3, while <a href="/wiki/Drew_Hall" title="Drew Hall">Drew Hall</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rolando_Roomes" title="Rolando Roomes">Rolando Roomes</a> were <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_transactions#Options" title="Major League Baseball transactions">optioned</a> to Iowa; at the time, he had a 1.54 ERA and two saves in Triple-A ball.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> On May 7, Capel made his MLB debut: he pitched the final 1.2 innings of a game against the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Giants" title="San Francisco Giants">San Francisco Giants</a>, allowing no runs and no <a href="/wiki/Hit_(baseball)" title="Hit (baseball)">hits</a> but issuing a <a href="/wiki/Base_on_balls" title="Base on balls">walk</a> in a 2–1 Cubs loss.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> His first career win came the following day against the Giants, although he <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_baseball_(B)#blown_save" title="Glossary of baseball (B)">blew a save</a> by giving up a two-run <a href="/wiki/Home_run" title="Home run">home run</a> to <a href="/wiki/Bob_Brenly" title="Bob Brenly">Bob Brenly</a> and lost a 5–4 lead. The Cubs ultimately won 13–7.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Capel continued to pitch for the club until June 30, when the Cubs optioned him to Iowa to open a roster spot for <a href="/wiki/Goose_Gossage" title="Goose Gossage">Rich "Goose" Gossage</a> to come off the 15-day <a href="/wiki/Disabled_list" class="mw-redirect" title="Disabled list">disabled list</a> (DL).<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He returned to the MLB club on August 8 to replace Schiraldi, but was demoted on August 13 before making an appearance, as Chicago activated <a href="/wiki/Les_Lancaster" title="Les Lancaster">Les Lancaster</a> from the DL.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Capel rejoined the club on August 31, and pitched in five more games before the season's end.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Over his first MLB season, Capel pitched in 20 games and allowed 16 <a href="/wiki/Earned_run" title="Earned run">earned runs</a> over 29.1 innings for a 4.91 ERA.<sup id="cite_ref-Bref_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bref-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He pitched the entire 1989 season for Iowa, appearing in 64 games for the team.<sup id="cite_ref-Minors_21-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Minors-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> On October 15, Capel was granted free agency by the Cubs; he signed with the Brewers two months later.<sup id="cite_ref-Bref_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bref-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Milwaukee_Brewers">Milwaukee Brewers</span></h3>
<p>Capel competed against 22 other pitchers for an MLB roster spot in the Brewers' spring training camp.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> The Brewers optioned Capel to their Triple-A affiliate, the <a href="/wiki/Denver_Zephyrs" title="Denver Zephyrs">Denver Zephyrs</a>, on April 5, before the start of Milwaukee's 1990 season.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> On May 17, Denver placed Capel on the DL with a strained <a href="/wiki/Achilles_tendon" title="Achilles tendon">Achilles tendon</a>, which opened a roster spot on the Zephyrs and allowed the Brewers to send <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Navarro" title="Jaime Navarro">Jaime Navarro</a> back to Denver.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> When the Brewers needed a replacement for the injured <a href="/wiki/Bill_Wegman" title="Bill Wegman">Bill Wegman</a>, Capel was called up to the major leagues.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> For his Brewers debut, Capel faced five batters and allowed four runs (two charged to Capel) against the <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_Orioles" title="Baltimore Orioles">Baltimore Orioles</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> His strikeout of <a href="/wiki/Billy_Ripken" title="Billy Ripken">Billy Ripken</a> was the only MLB <a href="/wiki/Out_(baseball)" title="Out (baseball)">out</a> Capel recorded in 1990. On June 8, he made his second and final outing with the Brewers and allowed four runs (three charged to Capel) against the <a href="/wiki/Toronto_Blue_Jays" title="Toronto Blue Jays">Toronto Blue Jays</a>; combined, batters hit .857 against Capel as he finished the season with a 135.00 ERA.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> When <a href="/wiki/Greg_Vaughn" title="Greg Vaughn">Greg Vaughn</a>, returning from a <a href="/wiki/Metatarsophalangeal_joint_sprain" title="Metatarsophalangeal joint sprain">turf toe</a> injury and a sprained left ankle, was activated from the DL on June 11, Capel was sent to Triple-A.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> He finished the season with Denver where he had a 4–3 record with a 4.26 ERA.<sup id="cite_ref-Minors_21-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Minors-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> On October 4, the Brewers granted Capel free agency; he signed with the Astros on January 5, 1991.<sup id="cite_ref-Minors_21-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Minors-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Houston_Astros">Houston Astros</span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:25%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"All I want is another chance. I'm not a flashy pitcher. I'm not outstanding. But I have knowledge for the game and I respond to pressure situations. Too often teams look at a pitcher's velocity, his size and what round he was taken when they consider signing him. They don't look at you as a person. I'm looking for that chance to meet someone who would recognize me as a pitcher who has the right makeup. I believe I can be an asset to any club."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">— Mike Capel on his 1992 season<sup id="cite_ref-All_Star_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-All_Star-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Capel began his 1991 season with the Triple-A <a href="/wiki/Tucson_Toros" title="Tucson Toros">Tucson Toros</a>; the Astros added Capel to their MLB roster on June 7. Two days later, against the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Mets" title="New York Mets">New York Mets</a>, Capel allowed the game-winning home run to <a href="/wiki/Howard_Johnson_(baseball)" title="Howard Johnson (baseball)">Howard Johnson</a> in the top of the 11th inning.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Against the Mets on June 14, Capel, starting pitcher <a href="/wiki/Pete_Harnisch" title="Pete Harnisch">Pete Harnisch</a>, and closer <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clancy_(baseball)" title="Jim Clancy (baseball)">Jim Clancy</a> combined for a four-hitter.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> His final MLB appearance came on August 14 against the <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Padres" title="San Diego Padres">San Diego Padres</a>, when he pitched one inning in relief of <a href="/wiki/Jim_Corsi_(baseball)" title="Jim Corsi (baseball)">Jim Corsi</a> in a 4–1 loss.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> Capel said pitching for Houston was "the high point of [his] career".<sup id="cite_ref-All_Star_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-All_Star-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Over 25 games pitched, Capel finished the season with a 1–3 record and a 3.03 ERA.<sup id="cite_ref-Bref_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bref-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>To reduce their roster to 35 players, the Astros assigned Capel and seven others to the Toros prior to the <a href="/wiki/1992_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="1992 Major League Baseball season">1992 MLB season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-All_Star_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-All_Star-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> Capel was selected to the <a href="/wiki/Triple-A_All-Star_Game" title="Triple-A All-Star Game">Triple-A All-Star Game</a> to replace <a href="/wiki/Tim_Scott_(baseball)" title="Tim Scott (baseball)">Tim Scott</a>, whom the Padres promoted to MLB. He told Javier Morales of the <i><a href="/wiki/Arizona_Daily_Star" title="Arizona Daily Star">Arizona Daily Star</a></i> "this [was] one of the greatest thrills of [his] career," and that he hoped it showed the Astros he could pitch in MLB.<sup id="cite_ref-All_Star_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-All_Star-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Doug_Jones_(baseball)" title="Doug Jones (baseball)">Doug Jones</a> worked as Houston's closer in 1992, however, and Capel never played for the MLB club.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> For the Toros, Capel had a 6–6 record and a 2.19 ERA with a team-leading 18 saves.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> The following season, the <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Expos" title="Montreal Expos">Montreal Expos</a> invited Capel to their spring camp as a non-roster invitee.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Capel elected to remain with the Astros organization where he allowed 26 earned runs in 32.2 innings and pitched the entire season in Triple-A.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> The Toros, managed by <a href="/wiki/Rick_Sweet" title="Rick Sweet">Rick Sweet</a>, finished first in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Coast_League" title="Pacific Coast League">Pacific Coast League</a> (PCL) South Division with an 83–60 record and made the playoffs.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Four_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Four-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> Unlike the 1993 MLB playoffs, in which four teams competed, only two teams could make the PCL playoffs. In the championship series, the Toros faced the <a href="/wiki/Portland_Beavers" title="Portland Beavers">Portland Beavers</a>, champions of the North Division and holders of a PCL-best 87–56 record.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> In a best-of-seven series, the Toros defeated the Beavers four games to two.<sup id="cite_ref-Four_59-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Four-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> After the season, the Astros did not re-sign Capel.<sup id="cite_ref-Bref_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bref-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> While playing in MLB, Capel stood at 6&#160;feet 1&#160;inch (185&#160;cm) and weighed 175 pounds (79&#160;kg).<sup id="cite_ref-Bref_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bref-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span></h2>
<p>In February 1986, Capel pitched in the University of Texas Alumni v. Varsity game, which ended in a 14–14 tie.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> Capel started to use <a href="/wiki/Dipping_tobacco" title="Dipping tobacco">dipping tobacco</a> in 1976 and in March 1988 had a non-cancerous lesion removed from his lip; he tried to quit the product in March 1988.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> Capel met his wife, Elizabeth, at Roger Clemens' wedding.<sup id="cite_ref-All_Star_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-All_Star-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Outside of baseball, he is a close friend of Clemens, and during Clemens' <a href="/wiki/Roger_Clemens#Steroid_use_accusations" title="Roger Clemens">2012 perjury trial</a>, Capel testified on his work ethic and character.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> In 2012, Mike worked as the general manager of a car dealership in Houston.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> Capel's son, <a href="/wiki/Conner_Capel" class="mw-redirect" title="Conner Capel">Conner</a>, played baseball as an <a href="/wiki/Outfielder" title="Outfielder">outfielder</a> for <a href="/wiki/Seven_Lakes_High_School" title="Seven Lakes High School">Seven Lakes High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Katy,_Texas" title="Katy, Texas">Katy, Texas</a>, where, in his senior year, he had a .456 <a href="/wiki/Batting_average_(baseball)" title="Batting average (baseball)">batting average</a> with 23 <a href="/wiki/Stolen_base" title="Stolen base">stolen bases</a> and 36 <a href="/wiki/Run_(baseball)" title="Run (baseball)">runs scored</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Son_draft_67-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Son_draft-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Indians" title="Cleveland Indians">Cleveland Indians</a> drafted Conner in the fifth round of the <a href="/wiki/2016_Major_League_Baseball_draft" title="2016 Major League Baseball draft">2016 MLB draft</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Son_draft_67-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Son_draft-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
